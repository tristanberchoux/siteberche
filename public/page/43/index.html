Why do we still teach a semester of trigonometry? How about engineering instead?

Rafael Irizarry 2013/10/11

Arthur Benjamini says we should teach statistics before calculus. He points out that most of what we do in high school math is preparing us for calculus. He makes the point that while physicists, engineers and economists need calculus, in the digital age, discrete math, probability and statistics are much more relevant to everyone else. I agree with him and was happy to see Statistics as part of the common core.
