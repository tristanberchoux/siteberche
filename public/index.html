Landhood

Landscapes • Rural livelihoods • Territorial development


I am a lecturer in rural geography at the Mediterranean Agronomic Institute of Montpellier in France (CIHEAM-IAMM). My principal research interests lie in the field of spatial planning and sustainable rural development. My current work looks at the impact of climate change on landscapes and livelihoods under different policy scenarios using spatial modelling. I have a particular expertise in quantitative modelling applied to rural territories from a social, environmental and economic perspective.

Research interests

  • Economic geography (livelihoods, employment, territorial impact of climate change, quantitative modelling of rural territories, political economy of livelihoods)
  • Landscape planning and land systems (policies, management, rural development)
  • Agricultural economics and policies (agricultural prices, markets, family farming, microfinance, migration)
