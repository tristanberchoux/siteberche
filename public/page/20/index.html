We need a statistically rigorous and scientifically meaningful definition of replication

Rafael Irizarry 2015/10/20

Replication and confirmation are indispensable concepts that help define scientific facts. However, the way in which we reach scientific consensus on a given finding is rather complex. Although some press releases try to convince us otherwise, rarely is one publication enough. In fact, most published results go unnoticed and no attempts to replicate them are made. These are not debunked either; they simply get discarded to the dustbin of history. The very few results that garner enough attention for others to spend time and energy on them are assessed by an ad-hoc process involving a community of peers.
