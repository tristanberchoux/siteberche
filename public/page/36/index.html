On the scalability of statistical procedures: why the p-value bashers just don't get it.

Jeff Leek 2014/02/14

Executive Summary The problem is not p-values it is a fundamental shortage of data analytic skill. In general it makes sense to reduce researcher degrees of freedom for non-experts, but any choice of statistic, when used by many untrained people, will be flawed. The long term solution is to require training in both statistics and data analysis for anyone who uses data but particularly journal editors, reviewers, and scientists in molecular biology, medicine, physics, economics, and astronomy.
